Defining the Corn-Cubit

The protocol is built upon a hierarchical, biologically-derived measurement system. This structure allows for both macro-level field planning and micro-level seed placement to be described in the same intuitive language, which is as understandable to a machine as it is to a farmer with a measuring rod.

Hierarchical Breakdown

1 Corn-Cubit

A canonical 10-foot corn stalk.

1 Foot

1/10th of a Corn-Cubit.

1 Cob-Length

A subdivision for row spacing.

1 Kernel-Width

The smallest unit for precision planting depth and spacing.
